Resources on Intersectionality and Implicit Bias

In today’s increasingly diverse society, understanding the concepts of intersectionality and implicit bias is essential for fostering a healthy society. Intersectionality, a term created by the legal scholar Kimberle Crenshaw, showcases how the overlapping social identities—such as race, gender, class, and sexuality—interact to shape that person's experience of discrimination or privilege. On the other hand, implicit bias refers to the unconscious attitudes or stereotypes that influence our perceptions and behaviors towards others.
